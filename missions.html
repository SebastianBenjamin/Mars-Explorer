Mars Missions

A journey through humankind’s exploration of the Red Planet

Mariner 4 (1964)

Launch Date: November 28, 1964

Landing Date: N/A (Flyby)

Mission Goal: First successful flyby of Mars and the first to send back close-up images of the Martian surface.

Mariner 6 (1969)

Launch Date: February 24, 1969

Landing Date: N/A (Flyby)

Mission Goal: First detailed images of Mars’ surface to provide greater understanding of its atmosphere and geology.

Mariner 7 (1969)

Launch Date: March 27, 1969

Landing Date: N/A (Flyby)

Mission Goal: To send back more images from a flyby and further study Mars’ surface features and atmosphere.

Viking 1 (1975)

Launch Date: August 20, 1975

Landing Date: July 20, 1976

Mission Goal: First successful landing on Mars and comprehensive study of its surface, atmosphere, and search for signs of life.

Viking 2 (1976)

Launch Date: September 9, 1975

Landing Date: September 3, 1976

Mission Goal: Second successful landing on Mars to search for life and study its geology and atmosphere.

Mars Global Surveyor (1996)

Launch Date: November 7, 1996

Landing Date: N/A (Orbiter)

Mission Goal: Orbited Mars for 9 years mapping its surface, studying its geology and atmosphere, and searching for evidence of water.

Pathfinder / Sojourner (1997)

Launch Date: December 4, 1996

Landing Date: July 4, 1997

Mission Goal: Delivered the Sojourner rover to Mars, allowing for the first mobile exploration of the planet’s surface.

Mars Odyssey (2001)

Launch Date: April 7, 2001

Landing Date: N/A (Orbiter)

Mission Goal: Search for water ice, study Mars’ weather and geology, and relay communications between Earth and Mars missions.

Spirit Rover (2003)

Launch Date: June 10, 2003

Landing Date: January 4, 2004

Mission Goal: To explore Mars’ surface and analyze rocks and soil for signs of past water.

Opportunity Rover (2003)

Launch Date: July 7, 2003

Landing Date: January 25, 2004

Mission Goal: Twin rover to Spirit, it explored the opposite side of Mars and discovered signs of ancient water activity on the planet.

Mars Express (2003)

Launch Date: June 2, 2003

Landing Date: N/A (Orbiter)

Mission Goal: A European Space Agency mission, Mars Express orbited Mars to study its surface and atmosphere with high-resolution imaging and data collection.

Mars Reconnaissance Orbiter (2005)

Launch Date: August 12, 2005

Landing Date: N/A (Orbiter)

Mission Goal: To conduct detailed imaging of Mars' surface and atmosphere and support other Mars missions with data and communications.

Phoenix Mars Lander (2007)

Launch Date: August 4, 2007

Landing Date: May 25, 2008

Mission Goal: To land near the northern ice cap and study the soil, atmosphere, and search for evidence of water ice and past habitability.

Curiosity Rover (2012)

Launch Date: November 26, 2011

Landing Date: August 6, 2012

Mission Goal: To study the climate and geology of Mars, assess whether the planet has ever supported microbial life, and investigate its water history.

Mars Atmosphere and Volatile EvolutioN (MAVEN) (2013)

Launch Date: November 18, 2013

Landing Date:September 21, 2014

Mission Goal:The Mars Atmosphere and Volatile EvolutioN (MAVEN) mission will determine how much of the Martian atmosphere has been lost over time by measuring the current rate of escape to space and gathering enough information about the relevant processes to allow extrapolation backward in time.

InSight Lander (2018)

Launch Date: May 5, 2018

Landing Date: November 26, 2018

Mission Goal: To study Mars’ interior, including its seismic activity and heat flow, to better understand the planet's geology.

Perseverance Rover (2020)

Launch Date: July 30, 2020

Landing Date: February 18, 2021

Mission Goal: To explore the Jezero Crater for signs of ancient life, collect samples for future return to Earth, and demonstrate new technology like the Ingenuity helicopter.

Ingenuity Mars Helicopter (2020)

Launch Date: July 30, 2020

Landing Date: February 18, 2021

Mission Goal: To demonstrate the feasibility of powered, controlled flight on Mars in its thin atmosphere.
